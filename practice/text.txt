Display Properties in CSS:

1. display: block;
- Elements with display: block start on a new line and take up the full width available.
- Examples: <div>, <h1>â€“<h6>, <p>
- Properties commonly used:
    - width, height
    - margin, padding
    - border

2. display: inline;
- Elements with display: inline do not start on a new line and only take up as much width as necessary.
- Examples: <span>, <a>, <strong>
- Properties commonly used:
    - margin-left, margin-right (top/bottom margins may not work)
    - padding-left, padding-right
    - width and height are ignored

3. display: flex;
- Enables a flex container for flexible layouts.
- Children become flex items and can be arranged in rows or columns.
- Properties for the flex container:
    - flex-direction: row | column | row-reverse | column-reverse
    - justify-content: flex-start | flex-end | center | space-between | space-around | space-evenly
    - align-items: stretch | flex-start | flex-end | center | baseline
    - flex-wrap: nowrap | wrap | wrap-reverse
- Properties for flex items:
    - flex-grow, flex-shrink, flex-basis
    - align-self
